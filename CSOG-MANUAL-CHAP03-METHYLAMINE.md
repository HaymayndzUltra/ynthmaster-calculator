# CSOG MANUAL: CHAPTER 3 - METHYLAMINE GENERATION
**PROTOCOL ID:** REAG-MA-03
**METHOD:** HEXAMINE HYDROLYSIS
**HAZARD:** EXTREME ODOR / FLAMMABILITY

---

## 1. THE CHEMISTRY (Why this works)
We are breaking down **Hexamine** (6 Carbons, 4 Nitrogens) using **Hydrochloric Acid**.
*   **Reaction:** Hexamine + HCl + H2O → Methylamine HCl + Ammonium Chloride + Formaldehyde.
*   **The Problem:** We want *Methylamine HCl*. We do NOT want *Ammonium Chloride* (useless salt) or *Formaldehyde* (toxic gas).

---

## 2. THE HYDROLYSIS (The Cook)

**INGREDIENTS:**
*   **100g Hexamine** (Esbit Tablets, crushed to powder).
*   **300mL Muriatic Acid** (32% HCl). Or use 150mL Lab Grade HCl + 150mL Water.

**SETUP:**
*   Round Bottom Flask or Beaker.
*   **REQUIRED:** Fume Hood or Outside Setup. The Formaldehyde gas burns eyes/lungs.

**PROCEDURE:**
1.  **Mixing:** Place crushed Hexamine in the flask. Pour in the Acid.
    *   *Observation:* It might fizz slightly.
2.  **Heating:** Bring to a **GENTLE SIMMER** (approx 80-90°C). Do NOT boil violently.
3.  **Time:** Simmer for **3 to 4 hours**.
    *   *Visual Cue:* The liquid will start clear/cloudy. As it cooks, a white solid (Ammonium Chloride) will precipitate (fall to bottom) because it is less soluble in hot acid than Methylamine.
    *   *Smell:* It will smell strongly of Formaldehyde (pickles/embalming fluid).
4.  **Concentration:** After 4 hours, verify that a lot of white sludge is at the bottom.

---

## 3. FILTRATION (Removing the Trash)

**OBJECTIVE:** Remove Ammonium Chloride. Keep the Liquid (Methylamine).

1.  **Hot Filter:** While the liquid is still **HOT** (approx 60-70°C), pour it through a filter (Coffee filter or Laboratory Paper).
    *   *Why Hot?* Methylamine HCl stays dissolved in hot liquid. Ammonium Chloride does not. If you let it cool, *everything* crystallizes together (Bad).
2.  **The Solid:** The white sludge in the filter is Ammonium Chloride. **THROW IT AWAY.**
3.  **The Liquid:** The clear yellow/liquid that passed through contains your Methylamine.

---

## 4. CRYSTALLIZATION (Harvesting)

**OBJECTIVE:** Turn the liquid into Methylamine HCl crystals.

1.  **Evaporation:** Put the filtered liquid in a Pyrex dish/pot. Heat gently to evaporate the water/acid.
    *   **WARNING:** Do not burn it. Use a steam bath or low electric heat.
2.  **The Sludge:** As it dries, it turns into a thick yellow/white sludge.
3.  **Purification (The Alcohol Wash):**
    *   Get the dry-ish sludge.
    *   Add **Hot Isopropanol (99%)** or **Ethanol**. Use *just enough* to cover it.
    *   Boil the alcohol with the sludge.
    *   *Chemistry:* Methylamine dissolves in hot alcohol. Ammonium Chloride does NOT.
4.  **Final Filter:** Filter the hot alcohol.
    *   **Solid in Filter:** Remaining Ammonium Chloride (Trash).
    *   **Liquid:** Alcohol + Pure Methylamine.
5.  **Cooling:** Put the Alcohol liquid in the freezer (-20°C) or let sit.
    *   *Result:* Beautiful clear/white crystals will grow. These are **Pure Methylamine HCl**.
6.  **Dry:** Filter out the crystals. Let them dry in air.

---

## 5. VERIFICATION (Is it real?)

**TEST 1: THE MELTING POINT**
*   **Methylamine HCl:** Melts at **225°C - 230°C**.
*   **Ammonium Chloride:** Does not melt. It sublimes (turns to smoke) at **338°C**.
*   *Action:* Put a tiny crystal on foil. Heat. If it melts into a puddle around 230°C, it's good. If it just smokes at very high heat, it's trash (Ammonium).

**TEST 2: THE SMELL TEST**
*   Mix a tiny pinch of crystal with a drop of Lye (NaOH) water.
*   **Methylamine:** Smells like **ROTTING FISH** (Fishy/Ammonia).
*   **Ammonium:** Smells like **URINE** (Pure Ammonia).

**TEST 3: THE BURN TEST**
*   Throw a pinch in a fire.
*   **Methylamine:** Burns with a yellow flame (It is organic).
*   **Ammonium:** Does not burn. It just smokes.

---

## 6. SAFETY & FAILURE MODES

| Hazard | Mitigation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Formaldehyde Gas** | Generated during hydrolysis. Causes blindness/lung damage. **MUST** use gas mask or do outdoors. |
| **Fish Smell** | Methylamine smell sticks to clothes/walls. Use the **Negative Pressure Box** (Chapter 1) for all drying steps. |
| **Yellow Crystals** | Means traces of formaldehyde/impurities. Wash with cold Acetone to whiten. |
| **No Crystals (Goo)** | You didn't dry it enough. Water prevents crystallization. Heat longer (gently). |
