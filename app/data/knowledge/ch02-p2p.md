# CHAPTER 2: P2P SYNTHESIS
**METHOD:** LEAD ACETATE DRY DISTILLATION
**DIFFICULTY:** MEDIUM (Requires Heat Control)

## 1. REAGENT PREPARATION (The Drying Phase)

**OBJECTIVE:** Create "Lead Phenylacetate" by fusing the two chemicals.
**CRITICAL:** Water is the enemy. Any moisture left will turn into steam in the flask and cause an explosion or "puking" (foaming over).

**INGREDIENTS:**
* **136g Phenylacetic Acid (PAA)** (White crystals/powder).
* **325g Lead Acetate Trihydrate** (White crystals).

**PROCEDURE:**
1. **Mixing:** In a stainless steel pot (or Pyrex dish), mix the PAA and Lead Acetate.
2. **Dissolving:** Add a *small amount* of distilled water (just enough to make a wet slush/paste). This helps them mix chemically.
3. **Evaporation (The Dangerous Part):**
   * Heat the pot on a stove (Low-Medium heat). **Ventilation is mandatory** (Vinegar fumes).
   * Stir constantly with a metal/glass spoon.
   * *Visual Cue 1:* The mix will melt into a clear liquid first.
   * *Visual Cue 2:* As water leaves, it will turn white and pasty.
   * *Visual Cue 3:* It will dry into a solid white hard mass.
4. **Bone Drying:**
   * Break up the clumps. Keep heating gently.
   * **SUCCESS VISUAL:** The powder must be **Dusty White**. No steam rising. No wet spots.
   * **FAILURE MODE:** If it turns brown/black here, you burned it. Start over.
5. **Grinding:** Once cool, grind into a fine white powder (like flour).

## 2. THE DRY DISTILLATION (The Reaction)

**OBJECTIVE:** Pyrolyze the powder to release P2P oil.
**SETUP:** Distillation Flask (1L) -> Condenser -> Receiver Flask. **SAND BATH REQUIRED.**

**PROCEDURE:**
1. **Loading:** Fill the flask only **halfway** with the powder. Do not overfill (risk of clogging).
2. **Heating Profile (Gas Burner/Sand Bath):**
   * **T = 0 mins:** Turn heat to Medium. Sand heats up.
   * **T = 10 mins (100°C):** You might see condensation (water drops) in the condenser. This is residual moisture. **Ignore/Discard.**
   * **T = 20 mins (150°C):** The white powder starts to melt and bubble.
   * **T = 30 mins (200°C - 250°C):** **CRITICAL ZONE.**
     * *Visual Cue:* The flask fills with **White/Grey Fog**.
     * *Action:* Strong bubbling (CO2 release).
     * *Result:* An **Orange/Yellow Oil** starts dripping into the receiver.
3. **Monitoring:**
   * Maintain heat so the bubbling is steady but NOT violent.
   * **FAILURE MODE (Foaming):** If the bubbles rise up the neck of the flask, **REMOVE HEAT IMMEDIATELY**. Let it settle. Heat again slowly.
4. **Completion:**
   * Continue heating until no more oil drips over.
   * The residue in the flask will turn into a dry, black/grey solid (Lead/Lead Oxide).
   * **STOP** when only black solid remains and no vapor rises.

## 3. CRITICAL CHECKPOINT (Verification)

**IS IT P2P OR GARBAGE?**

* **Look:** Should be a clear Yellow to Orange oil. If it's thick black tar, you overheated it.
* **Smell:**
  * **P2P:** Distinct floral smell. Like "Wilting Flowers" or "Apples mixed with Chemicals".
  * **Acetone:** Nail polish remover smell (This is a byproduct, it's okay).
  * **Burnt:** Smells like acrid smoke (You burned it).
* **Fluidity:** Should pour like vegetable oil. Not thick like motor oil.

## 4. PURIFICATION (The Cleanup)

**OBJECTIVE:** Remove Acetone, Water, and Acid residues.

**PROCEDURE:**
1. **The Wash:** Pour the Crude Oil into a Separatory Funnel.
2. **Lye Wash:** Add **10% NaOH Solution** (10g Lye in 100g Water).
   * *Action:* Shake well for 2 mins. Vent the gas.
   * *Observation:* The P2P oil will float (Top). The dirty water will sink (Bottom).
   * *Separation:* Drain the bottom layer (Waste). Keep the top oil.
3. **Water Wash:** Add Distilled Water to the oil. Shake. Drain water.
4. **Salt Wash (Optional):** Add Saturated Salt Water (Brine). Shake. Drain water. This pulls out moisture.

**FINAL RESULT:**
You now have **Crude P2P Oil**.
* **Purity:** ~70-80%.
* **Contains:** Some Dibenzyl Ketone.
* **Decision:** You can use this directly for the Al/Hg reaction (Chapter 4), OR you can distill it again to get pure clear P2P (Recommended for better crystals).

## 5. TROUBLESHOOTING & FAILURE MODES

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"Flask broke!"** | Water hit hot glass OR Thermal shock. | Use a **Sand Bath** (Metal pan with sand) to spread heat evenly. Never flame directly on glass. |
| **"It puked foam into the condenser."** | Heating too fast. | Stop heat. Let drain back. Heat slower. |
| **"White solid stuck in condenser."** | Acetone/Water mix freezing or Phenylacetic Acid subliming. | Turn off condenser water briefly to let hot vapor melt it down. |
| **"Yield is tiny (<20g)."** | Leaks in the system. | Teflon tape ALL joints. P2P vapor escapes easily. |
